# Qipao-Silhouette-LoRA-Series
Historical Qipao (Cheongsam) Silhouette LoRA Series for Flux

Historical Qipao Silhouette Evolution: Flux LoRA Series
Welcome to the Historical Qipao (Cheongsam) Silhouette LoRA Series, meticulously trained for the Flux.1 [dev] model.

Unlike conventional datasets that focus merely on oriental patterns or generalized aesthetics, this project is dedicated to the structural evolution and authentic physical tailoring of the Qipao across different decades. By employing rigorous, structure-based captioning, these LoRAs empower creators to generate historically accurate garments, capturing the exact transition from traditional flat-cutting to modern Western-influenced 3D tailoring.

🕰️ The Collections
1. The 1920s & 1930s: The Classical Slender Era
This model focuses on the traditional flat-cutting techniques of the early Republican era.

Silhouette: Natural shoulders, form-fitting but relying on the fabric's natural drape, and the iconic ankle-length straight or subtle S-curve.

Key Structural Details: Highly accurate rendering of traditional closure styles, including the Square Flap (square flap) and Curved Flap (curved flap), along with meticulously generated knot buttons (multiple buttons) and contrasting trims (contrasting trim).

2. The 1950s: The Golden Hourglass
Capturing the profound influence of Western "New Look" tailoring on traditional Chinese dress.

Silhouette: The extreme sculpted hourglass figure (hourglass figure, sculpted silhouette).

Key Structural Details: Accurately reproduces 3D tailoring elements such as bust and waist darts (darts, tailored), cinched waists (cinched waist), structured shoulders, and the practical mid-calf length (mid-calf length), reflecting the modernization of the garment.
⚙️ Recommended Inference Settings (Crucial for Flux)
To achieve the best structural accuracy without burning the image, please adhere to the following settings in ComfyUI, Forge, or your preferred UI:

Base Model: Flux.1 [dev]

LoRA Weight: 0.6 - 0.8 (Adjust based on desired intensity)

CFG Scale: 1.0 (Do not use standard SDXL CFG values)

Flux Guidance: 3.5 - 4.0 (3.5 is the sweet spot for crisp tailoring details)

Sampling Steps: 20 - 30
